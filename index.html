Trends in COVID 19 Case Increases & Key Hospital Metrics

Created by Kara Haberstock Tanoue

6/28/2020

7-Day Average Daily Case Increases in Arizona & Pima County

Average daily case increases in Arizona and Pima County have rapidly increased since Memorial Day Weekend. Tuesday, June 23, had a record single-day case increase in Pima County (502) and Arizona (3,591).

Note that typically it takes 10-14 days to see the effects of major policy changes or events. This is due to both the incubation period of COVID-19 (median of 4-5 days between exposure and symptom onset according to the CDC) and the reporting lag for new case data (typically 4-7 days according to ADHS).

Average daily cases increases began to reliably increase beginning on May 29, which was 14 days following the expiration of stay at home orders in Arizona.

Key Dates in Arizona

03/15: Schools Close
03/31: Stay at Home Order goes into effect
04/23: Testing Expands
05/15: Stay at Home Order expires
05/25: Memorial Day

The 7-Day Average Daily Case Increase variable is a derived variable obtained by averaging the new cases added each day over a rolling 7-day window. A 7-day window was used as there are strong day of the week effects in data reporting.

Case-to-Test Ratios

The Case-to-Test ratio has been steadily rising for both Arizona and Pima County, indicating that the increase in cases is not simply due to increased testing.

The Case-to-Test Ratio is the ratio of confirmed COVID-19 cases to the total number of PCR tests administered. If the number of cases is increasing due to increased testing, we expect this ratio to remain constant. A rising case-to-test ratio indicates accelerating spread of COVID-19 or insufficient testing.

Key Hospital Metrics

Hospital COVID-19 specific metrics have been rapidly trending up in the last 3 weeks across Arizona. Hospital in-patient bed usage is currently at 86% and ICU bed usage is at 87% statewide (note that beds in use include both COVID and non-COVID patients).

All hospital metrics are collected by ADHS through the EMResource platform. The data reflect hospital reported bed usage and resource availability at 5:00 PM the preceding day.

Data & Methods:

All data on COVID-19 case counts and hospital metrics are drawn from the Arizona Department of Health Services (ADHS) COVID-19 Data Dashboard, accessed at https://www.azdhs.gov/preparedness/epidemiology-disease-control/infectious-disease-epidemiology/covid-19/dashboards/index.php.
